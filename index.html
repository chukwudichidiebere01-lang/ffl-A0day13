Part 3 — Les Nombres 70 à 200
The tricky numbers · Platform numbers · Prices · Times
📌 TEF/TCF Canada: Numbers 70–100 are notoriously tricky for Anglophones. TEF listening tasks ALWAYS include them in prices, times, and platform numbers. Master the pattern — don't just memorise!
👩🏾‍🏫
Coach Linda
"French numbers 70–99 do NOT follow the simple pattern you know. French uses soixante-dix (60+10) for 70, and quatre-vingts (4×20) for 80. It looks strange but once you see the pattern, it clicks. In a train station, you hear these numbers constantly — platform 82, price 97€, departure 17h45."
🔢 The pattern explained
60-69: soixante (60) + number → normal
70-79: soixante-dix (60+10) → soixante-onze, soixante-douze...
80: quatre-vingts (4×20) → note the s!
81-89: quatre-vingt-number → NO s on vingt!
90-99: quatre-vingt-dix (4×20+10) → quatre-vingt-onze...
100: cent | 101-199: cent + number | 200: deux cents
